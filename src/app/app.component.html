Scooter

"Scooter" is a portmanteau of "motor" and "pedal." It refers to a small, lightweight motorized bicycle that typically has a low-powered engine, often with a displacement less than 50cc. Mopeds usually feature both pedals for human-powered propulsion and a small engine for motorized assistance. They are popular for short-distance urban commuting due to their fuel efficiency and ease of maneuverability.

Superbike

A "superbike" refers to a high-performance and powerful motorcycle designed for racing or high-speed road use. These motorcycles are often characterized by their advanced engineering, cutting-edge technology, and powerful engines with large displacements. Superbikes are built for speed, agility, and precision on the race track, featuring aerodynamic designs and state-of-the-art components.

Motorcycle

A "motorcycle" is a two-wheeled motor vehicle powered by an engine, typically with a higher displacement than mopeds. Unlike mopeds, motorcycles are designed solely for motorized propulsion and do not include pedals for human-powered assistance. Motorcycles come in various styles, such as cruisers, sport bikes, touring bikes, and more, catering to different riding preferences and purposes.
